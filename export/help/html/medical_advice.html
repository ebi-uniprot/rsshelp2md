UniProtKB is a protein sequence database and our staff consists of biologists and biochemists that are not trained to give medical advice.

UniProtKB/Swiss-Prot contains a limited amount of information concerning genetic diseases (in the Pathology and Biotech section, Involvement in disease subsection), as well as sequence information on some disease-linked variations (see Sequence section, or Natural variant ' under 'Pathology and Biotech').

see:

  • How can I get all the proteins involved in a given disease?

Much more information on diseases is available from the OMIM database .

In case you are interested in a "rare" genetic disease you can look up the Orphanet website , which is cross-referenced from UniProtKB/Swiss-Prot entry pages and also has links to disease-specific patient organizations.

In addition, the following sites may be of help as they offer a variety of links to information sources on medical topics:

  • HON - The Health On the Net foundation server
  • MedicineNet - Medical Reference Site
  • MedSite Navigator - Medical and Biotech site
  • Medline Plus: NLM list of resources for health questions
  • The Medical Reference Resource Library
